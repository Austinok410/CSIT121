Why is it important?

JSON is popular for many reasons and sees widespread use, underscoring its importance. By providing a consistent, text‑based structure, it simplifies data exchange between different languages, frameworks, and devices.

Communication between clients and servers was not always as efficient as it is today. In the early days of the web, most pages were static, so a specialized data‑exchange format was unnecessary. As dynamic applications and complex back‑end services emerged, developers needed a lightweight, human‑readable way to send structured information and JSON filled that gap.

Because JSON is easy for both people and computers to read, it has become the go‑to format for REST and GraphQL calls, mobile apps, and even everyday files like package.json. Its short syntax keeps network messages small, which saves bandwidth and speeds up pages—especially on phones.
