Lab 01: Student Bio

About Me

My name is Austin Okafor. I'm a 29-year-old Nigerian American residing in the lovely city of Baltimore. I try not to take myself too seriously and, as a result, have been labeled as "funny" by all my friends. I DJ full-time and have been blessed with opportunities to perform at Sony's headquarters in New York and to tour in London as the DJ for the artist Sarina on R&B singer Luna Elle's tour. Before DJing became my career, I had always been passionate about music and have been producing for about 10 years. I have executive-produced several projects and even have placements with Sony-signed artist Lovemoor. Even before my love for music fully developed in high school, I always had a deep appreciation for technology. Whenever I visited a thrift store, I would immediately head to the electronics section, immersing myself in all the tech—from VHS players to radios. As I grew older, I became the designated "family technician," a role that followed me into my professional life when I worked in telecommunications as the Retail Operations Leader (a.k.a. store manager) at Cricket Wireless.

Education

My major at CCBC is Information Technology. I am currently enrolled in two courses: CSIT 101 and CSIT 121.

Skills/Hobbies

Saying that I love music would be an understatement. With that passion comes several pastimes I enjoy. For starters, I love to DJ. I am part of a collective of DJs called FTG (From the Garage), where we meet weekly to practice and refine our skills. I have also developed a passion for bowling. While I’m not rolling 200+ games just yet, it’s definitely a great stress reliever. Calisthenics is another hobby of mine. Doing random push-ups throughout the day helps keep me mentally focused—plus, the added bonus of bigger arms and a stronger chest doesn’t hurt.
